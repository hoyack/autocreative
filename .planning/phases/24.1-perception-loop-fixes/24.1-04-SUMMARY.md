## Decisions Made

- **Why shrink the title AND shift details (not one or the other)?**
  Shrinking the title alone (h 600 → 480) leaves a 700px gap (600 → 1300)
  which is more than enough on paper, but the renderer has no hard clip
  on bbox h — only width-based wrap. Shifting details up to y=1200
  reclaims 100px of dead space below the title and keeps the visual
  weight balanced. The 600px gap (y=600..1200) gives a clear "white space"
  band that any reasonable title wrap cannot bridge.
- **Why sha256 tripwire over `git diff --stat`?** The tripwire fails in
  CI automatically on any drift to the other 5 schemas, while the
  diff-stat approach requires a human in the review loop. For a "do not
  modify these files" contract, automated enforcement is strictly better.
- **Why >=240px gap assertion** in the bold_modern-specific test? Belt-
  and-suspenders. Even if a future edit accidentally bumps cover_title.h
  back up to 700 (above 480 cap), the 240px-gap assertion keeps the
  details band clear of the title bottom edge. The two assertions catch
  the same bug-class from different angles.
